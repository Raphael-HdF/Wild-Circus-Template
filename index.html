About Us

The Wild Circus came into being on 11th December 1852 thanks to the Duke of Morny (half-brother of Prince Louis-Napoléon), who was granted building permission and Jacques Hittorf, his architect. A few years later, on 12th November 1859, the circus was brought to life by the ‘flying trapeze', an act invented by Léotard, known as the ‘flying performer', from Toulouse.

At the Wild Circus, the enchanting shows follow one another but never look alike. This is the golden rule! Only excellence persists. The masters of the place watch over it; They have at heart to always combine excellence with elegance and flamboyance!

Ours spectacles offers unprecedented moments of pure magic with, for highlight, children and old's laughs fuses and shatters. The building echo with their laughter for the delight of the charismatic "Without Socks", our wonderful clowns.

When artists and animals invest this magical and mythical track, you can only be seduced and subdued.
